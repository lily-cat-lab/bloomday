New England Probability and Computing Seminar

The New England Probability and Computing Seminar (NES-PC) is an effort to bring together researchers working on applied probability and its applications in computer science. We will be scheduling talks on a rolling basis throughout Fall 2025. The organizers are primarily located in the greater Boston area so all times are in ET. The seminar is open to all and will be held remotely.

Attending

Open to all, including faculty and students from any educational institution. No registration is required.

When: Wednesdays, 5:00-6:00 PM Eastern Time
Where: Remote (Zoom)
Updates: Join our google group

Scope

NES-PC welcomes talks on all aspects of probability and computing, including but not limited to:

  • Probabilistic data structures including Bloom filters, cuckoo filters, and others
  • Randomized algorithms and streaming algorithms
  • Applications of probability including cryptography, machine learning, differential privacy
  • Performance modeling and analysis of systems using probability tools
  • Anything else that generally fits the theme of probability and computing

Submissions

To present your work at NES-PC, please submit a talk proposal by the end of the week before the seminar. Send proposals to nesbloom.organizers@gmail.com. Include your name, affiliation, short bio, talk title, and abstract. Talks are typically 1 hour, including Q&A. Optional links to papers, preprints, videos, or code are encouraged.

Schedule

The schedule will be updated as speakers confirm their presentations.

Notes

We particularly encourage submissions from researchers who belong to underrepresented groups including women, researchers from the Global South, LGBTQ+ researchers, and others. Our goal is creating an inclusive space beyond gender, race, ethnicity, national origin, or any other dimension of identity. Our message is clear: you are welcome and you matter!

We follow the ACM Code of Ethics.
