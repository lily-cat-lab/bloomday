New England Seminar on Bloom Filters

Overview

Welcome to the first annual New England Seminar on Bloom filters (NES-Bloom). We will be scheduling talks on a rolling basis throughout Fall 2025. The seminar will be held remotely via Zoom (link will be posted on the mailing list and Discord). The intent of NES-Bloom is to bring together researchers and practitioners across cs theory, algorithms, cryptography, and systems for talks and discussions.

Attending the seminar

Open to all, including faculty and students from any educational institution. No registration is required.

When: Wednesdays, 5:00-6:00 PM Eastern Time
Where: Remote (Zoom)
Mailing list: https://groups.google.com/g/nes-bloom

Scope of the seminar

NES-Bloom welcomes talks on all aspects of probabilistic data structures, including but not limited to:

  • Bloom filters and all its variants, including cuckoo filters, bloomier filters, and others
  • Count-Min Sketch, and other sketch-based data structures
  • Learning-augmented data structures such as the learned Bloom filter and others
  • Adversarial models and robustness related to data structures and streaming algorithms
  • Systems and applications using probabilistic data structures, including distributed systems, cloud computing, and others
  • Theoretical models and proofs, including the use of probabilistic data structures in theoretical models

Submission guidelines

To present your work at NES-Bloom, please submit a talk proposal by the end of the week before the seminar. You can send the proposal to nesbloom.organizers@gmail.com. The proposal should include your name, affiliation, short bio, talk title, abstract, and duration (Short is 30 minutes including Q&A, Long is 1 hour including Q&A). Optional links to papers, preprints, videos, or code.
