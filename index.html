New England Probability and Computing Seminar

Overview

Welcome to the first annual New England Probability and Computing Seminar (NES-PC). We will be scheduling talks on a rolling basis throughout Fall 2025. The seminar will be held remotely via Zoom (link will be posted on the mailing list and Discord). The intent of NES-PC is to bring together researchers and practitioners across cs theory, algorithms, cryptography, and systems for talks and discussions.

Attending the seminar

Open to all, including faculty and students from any educational institution. No registration is required.

When: Wednesdays, 5:00-6:00 PM Eastern Time
Where: Remote (Zoom)

Join our google group to get updates on the seminar.

Scope of the seminar

NES-PC welcomes talks on all aspects of probability and computing, including but not limited to:

  • Probabilistic data structures including Bloom filters, cuckoo filters, and others
  • Randomized algorithms and streaming algorithms
  • Applications of probability including cryptography, machine learning, differential privacy, and others
  • Performance modeling and analysis of systems using probability tools
  • Anything else that generally fits the theme of probability and computing

Submission guidelines

To present your work at NES-PC, please submit a talk proposal by the end of the week before the seminar. You can send the proposal to nesbloom.organizers@gmail.com. The proposal should include your name, affiliation, short bio, talk title, and abstract. Talks are typically 1 hour, including Q & A. Optional links to papers, preprints, videos, or code are encouraged. We will schedule talks on a rolling basis throughout Fall 2025.
